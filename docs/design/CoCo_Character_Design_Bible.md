# COCO
### Character & Class Progression Bible — Definitive Reference
*Companion document to the EDAS Game Design Bible*

---

## 0. Purpose of This Document

Your two reference sheets already contain the correct answer — a clean 8-stage progression (Novice → Explorer → Seeker → Apprentice → Scholar → Sage → Archivist → Keeper of Memories) with a consistent silhouette language, a growing gold-and-amethyst palette, and a legible expression set. This document exists to lock that down permanently: every color, silhouette beat, prop, and story trigger written here should be treated as **canon** going forward, so any artist, animator, or writer who touches CoCo produces something consistent, forever, without re-deriving the design from scratch each time.

**One naming note carried over from the main bible, kept intentionally:** stage 7 is called **Archivist** — the same title the villain claims for himself. This is not an accident to fix; it's one of the strongest quiet gut-punches available in the whole game (see §2.7), and it's already baked into your reference sheet's naming, so we keep it and build the story beat around it properly.

---

## 1. CORE IDENTITY

**Full name:** Cosima *(revealed only at the "Keeper of Memories" stage, in the True Ending)*
**Nickname:** CoCo — self-given, echoed from a lullaby fragment that never fully indexed (see EDAS Bible §4.2)
**Species/nature:** A living memory-echo of a real girl, given physical form by the fractured Edas world
**Core visual throughline across all 8 stages:**
- **Hair:** Golden blonde, always the brightest element in any frame she's in — this never changes color, only *volume, length, and styling complexity*, which is the primary visual language of her growth.
- **Eyes:** Warm amber-brown, large and expressive, consistent across every stage — CoCo's eyes are always fully "there," which is a deliberate choice: her *mind* is never missing, only her *memories* are. The eyes should never look empty or vacant, even at Novice.
- **Palette anchor:** Amethyst purple + soft cream/white + gold trim, present from stage 1, growing richer and more saturated at every stage rather than changing hue. Purple = Edas / memory / magic. Gold = restoration / light / truth. White/cream = the "surface world" self she's trying to get back to.
- **Silhouette rule:** Every stage must be recognizable in pure black silhouette at a glance — achieved through the growing complexity of cape/collar shapes, never through color alone (critical for mobile screen readability).

## 2. THE EIGHT STAGES

Each entry below is written as a full art-direction spec: **Visual**, **Palette**, **Silhouette Beat**, **Ability**, **Unlocks**, **Emotional State**, **Voice/Dialogue Note**, and **Animation Note**.

### 2.1 Novice — *"Unaware"*
**Visual:** Simple lavender-pink pinafore dress over a cream long-sleeve blouse, plain white ankle boots, hair in soft loose twin-tails with no accessories. A worn cream schoolbag/satchel crosses her chest — the single prop she starts with, symbolically "all she has left of herself." No cape, no cloak, no jewelry. She should read as small, plain, and slightly under-dressed compared to every later stage — the visual floor the whole arc rises from.
**Palette:** Pale dusty pink, cream, soft white. No gold yet — gold is *earned*, not given, and Novice is the only stage with none.
**Silhouette Beat:** Small, rounded, unadorned — twin-tails read as two small triangles, no cape volume at all.
**Ability — Spark of Curiosity:** Reveals hidden interactables in the immediate area with a soft pulse; the game's most basic "look closer" tool.
**Unlocks:** Basic interactions, Memory Notes (the earliest, barest version of the journal).
**Emotional State:** Gentle, quiet, a little lost — not sad, just *new*. She doesn't yet know enough about herself to be afraid of what she's missing.
**Voice/Dialogue Note:** Short sentences. Trails off often. Asks more questions than she answers.
**Animation Note:** Idle animation should include a small self-conscious habit — tugging the schoolbag strap, or looking down at her own hands like she's checking they're real.

### 2.2 Explorer — *"Awakening"*
**Visual:** Cream explorer-vest layered over the same blouse, now paired with a small chest-strap satchel with a visible "CoCo" tag, plain trousers-or-skirt-plus-boots silhouette, hair down and slightly fuller, catching more light. This is the first stage where she looks like she *chose* an outfit rather than simply having one.
**Palette:** Introduce the first true gold accents — trim on the vest, the satchel buckle — small, not yet dominant.
**Silhouette Beat:** Slightly wider stance, vest adds a defined waist-break to the silhouette; hair volume increases ~15%.
**Ability — Light Step:** Move through small hidden paths and gaps previously inaccessible.
**Unlocks:** Secret Areas, Field Notes (journal now records short world-lore, not just reminders).
**Emotional State:** The first flicker of *wanting* to know more, rather than passively wandering. Small, real excitement starts appearing in her expressions.
**Voice/Dialogue Note:** First jokes appear here — small, dry, slightly surprised at her own humor, as if she didn't know she had it in her.
**Animation Note:** Introduce a genuine "delighted" idle — a little bounce or spin when she solves something small, distinct from Novice's careful stillness.

### 2.3 Seeker — *"Searching"*
**Visual:** White dress with a soft violet collar and short cape-collar piece (the first cape-shaped silhouette element), satchel now worn lower and fuller, and — critically — she now **carries a glowing lantern**, her first held prop with light of its own rather than just reflecting the world's light.
**Palette:** White base, violet collar, warmer gold on the lantern's glow specifically (should be a slightly different, warmer gold than her trim gold, to read as "external light" vs. "her light").
**Silhouette Beat:** The short cape-collar is the first cape-shaped read; the lantern held low-right breaks the silhouette's symmetry in a readable, iconic way — this is a strong marketing/key-art pose stage.
**Ability — Echo Sense:** Detects nearby secrets and illusions; the lantern visibly flickers stronger near hidden content, giving the player a diegetic detector.
**Unlocks:** Puzzle Hints (first tier), Echo Vision (a temporary screen-filter that reveals fractured/illusory geometry).
**Emotional State:** Purposeful. She's stopped drifting and started *searching*, and it shows in posture — straighter spine, forward-leaning stance.
**Voice/Dialogue Note:** Starts asking the player pointed questions about the surface world — the first sign her curiosity is turning toward what she's lost, not just what's around her.
**Animation Note:** Lantern should have its own subtle secondary animation (gentle swing, light pulse) independent of her walk cycle — it should feel alive, almost like an early companion object.

### 2.4 Apprentice — *"Understanding"*
**Visual:** Full purple dress replaces white as the base color for the first time, cream blouse still visible at collar and cuffs, a proper belt/bag at the hip (upgraded from the shoulder satchel), and small floating book-pages begin to orbit her passively — the first stage with an ambient VFX signature, not just a held prop.
**Palette:** Purple becomes dominant for the first time; gold trim thickens along hems and cuffs.
**Silhouette Beat:** Dress fullness increases noticeably (A-line volume grows), floating pages add irregular silhouette "noise" at the edges — reads as magical/unstable in a good way, matching "Understanding" as a state that's still forming.
**Ability — Memory Link:** Connect symbols and activate mechanisms across a distance — her first *systemic* puzzle ability, not just a detection tool.
**Unlocks:** Memory Link puzzles world-wide, first companion NPC recruitment.
**Emotional State:** A visible confidence jump — this is the stage where CoCo starts finishing the player's sentences on things she "shouldn't" know (per EDAS Bible §5.1), and her design should reflect that new certainty: chin up, steadier hands.
**Voice/Dialogue Note:** This is where her humor gets its first real edge of sadness underneath it — jokes that land a half-second before she seems to notice they weren't entirely jokes.
**Animation Note:** The orbiting pages should slow and organize themselves (briefly forming neat stacks) during calm dialogue scenes, and scatter during combat/puzzle tension — an inexpensive but very readable "mental state" tell.

### 2.5 Scholar — *"Learning"*
**Visual:** The dress gains a structured bodice and a small flower hair-clip (her first personal accessory that isn't functional — pure self-expression), sleeves gain the beginnings of the folded gold cuff-bands that carry through to later stages, and she now holds a book actively rather than letting pages orbit passively — a shift from ambient magic to *deliberate* use of it.
**Palette:** Deeper, richer purple; gold now appears as clean structured trim lines (hems, cuffs, bodice seams) rather than scattered accents — the outfit starts to look "designed," not just decorated.
**Silhouette Beat:** Structured shoulders and bodice give a cleaner, more upright top-half silhouette; the flower clip adds a small, specific, instantly-recognizable head-silhouette detail useful for icon/UI work.
**Ability — Recall:** Restore and replay fragments of the past directly in the environment — first ability that manipulates *time*, not just space.
**Unlocks:** Lore Archives, Past Glimpses (short optional environmental flashback scenes).
**Emotional State:** Focused, a little more serious — this is the stage where grief starts entering her humor in earnest, per the main bible's mid-game emotional beat.
**Voice/Dialogue Note:** First stage where she can complete a full emotional thought out loud without deflecting into a joke immediately after.
**Animation Note:** Give her a new "reading" idle pose — she should occasionally open the book and genuinely read it unprompted, reinforcing that she's an active *scholar* now, not a passive vessel.

### 2.6 Sage — *"Realizing"*
**Visual:** Full cape returns and grows large and structured (echoing the Seeker's small cape-collar, now fully matured), a glowing orb replaces the book as her primary held prop, hair grows longer and gains soft wave/flow, and sparkle particles become a constant ambient presence around her rather than an occasional effect.
**Palette:** The richest, most saturated purple of the pre-final stages; gold now includes fine filigree detail work, not just clean trim lines — this is the stage where the outfit starts to look genuinely regal rather than simply "nice."
**Silhouette Beat:** Cape adds major volume and a strong triangular base to the silhouette; the held orb (rather than a book or lantern) reads as a distinctly more powerful, less "tool-like" prop.
**Ability — Time Weave:** Slow and align the flow of time in a local area — her most narratively significant ability yet, directly foreshadowing the endgame's time-fracture mechanics.
**Unlocks:** Time Puzzles, Alternate Routes (paths that only exist in certain time-states).
**Emotional State:** This is the "almost-whole but not quite" stage — she should look powerful and assured, but her expressions should retain a specific, deliberate flicker of uncertainty (a slightly-too-long pause before smiling) that keeps her from reading as fully resolved yet.
**Voice/Dialogue Note:** She starts speaking in first-person about memories she can't fully place ("I think I used to—") rather than the earlier third-person curiosity about the surface world in general.
**Animation Note:** The orb should very subtly dim during her uncertain-flicker beats and brighten during resolve — a small, cheap, constant emotional readout.

### 2.7 Archivist — *"Remembering"*
**Visual:** The most ornate pre-final robe: floor-length purple gown with a heavy structured cape, gold filigree now covering significant surface area (hems, collar, cuffs, a subtle circlet or hairpin suggestion at the crown of the head — not a full crown yet, intentionally held back for stage 8), hair fully long and flowing with soft magical lift. She now holds nothing — hands are free, open, or gently clasped — because by this stage her power is no longer *tool-based* at all.
**Palette:** Deep, near-royal purple; gold coverage is now the highest of any pre-final stage, but should stop short of stage 8's full radiance — there must be room left to grow.
**Silhouette Beat:** The largest, most complete silhouette before the final stage — full cape, full gown, full hair volume. Should read as "nearly a queen" without quite arriving.
**Ability — Archive Access:** Access sealed knowledge and forgotten doors — mechanically, the ability to open content that was locked for the entire rest of the game.
**Unlocks:** Sealed Archives, True Stories (the deepest, most direct lore content in the game).
**Emotional State — the design's most important beat:** This stage must be played with quiet, deliberate irony. CoCo now looks — visually and functionally — almost exactly like a smaller, gentler echo of the villain's own title and iconography. **This should be unsettling on a first viewing and devastating on a second one**, once the player has met Fen and understood what "Archivist" actually cost him. Do not let her look triumphant here. Let her look like someone standing very close to a door she doesn't fully know she should be afraid of.
**Voice/Dialogue Note:** For the first and only time, give her one line that echoes Fen's own phrasing from earlier cutscenes almost exactly, without either of them or the player commenting on it directly — pure environmental-storytelling dread, resolved only in stage 8.
**Animation Note:** Where every previous stage's idle has included a small nervous or curious tic, this is the first stage with a fully *still*, composed idle pose — deliberately the least "alive" animation in the sequence, right before the final stage becomes the most alive.

### 2.8 Keeper of Memories — *"Fully Remembered"*
**Visual:** The full regal transformation: an elaborate layered gown with the richest gold filigree in the entire design, a proper light circlet/crown motif woven into her hair (finally paying off the held-back detail from stage 7), a tall ornate staff replacing the orb as her final prop, and — the single most important addition — a soft radiant golden aura/halo effect surrounding her at all times, built from the same particle language as the game's lanterns and memory-motes throughout the whole world. She is, visually, made of the same light the player has been collecting the entire game.
**Palette:** The gold finally overtakes the purple as the dominant read at a distance — purple remains as the "base" color underneath, symbolically saying she is still herself, but she is now made primarily of light rather than shadow-and-light in balance.
**Silhouette Beat:** The largest, most ornate, most symmetrical silhouette in the set — wide gown, full crown-hair silhouette, staff held centered or slightly forward. This is the pose for final key art, the True Ending splash, and any box-art usage.
**Ability — Light of Restoration:** Heal corrupted memories and protect the timeline — her final ability is explicitly restorative and protective, never offensive, keeping her thematically distinct from a "power-up final form" cliché.
**Unlocks:** True Ending, The Whole Truth.
**Emotional State:** Fully present, fully warm, fully certain — and critically, this is the *only* stage where her expression sheet should include an unguarded, full, close-mouthed-eyes-closed smile (see §4), because it's the only point in the whole game where she's not holding anything back.
**Voice/Dialogue Note:** This is the only stage that gets to use her real name, Cosima, spoken aloud — and it should be spoken by someone else *to* her, not claimed by her for herself, keeping the moment about being seen rather than about self-declaration.
**Animation Note:** Every idle tic from every previous stage (the schoolbag tug, the bounce, the reading pose, the flicker-pause) should be very subtly, briefly quotable in her new idle loop — a full-circle animation callback that rewards players who've watched her closely the whole game, without any dialogue needing to point it out.

## 3. THE SPECTRAL / IN-BETWEEN STATE

Your first reference sheet (the three-figure purple-and-gold study) captures something the 8-stage sheet doesn't cover on its own: **the transition moments between stages**, and an **unstable/desynced alternate look**. Canonizing this as a third visual mode:

### 3.1 The Transition Flicker
Used only during the actual moment a Title is earned: a fully transparent, softly glowing gold-white silhouette of CoCo's *next* stage briefly overlays her current one, hands slightly raised, eyes closed, hair dissolving into drifting light-motes at the edges — exactly the leftmost figure in the reference sheet. This should last no more than 2–3 seconds and always resolve into the new stage's solid model. It is the single VFX moment in the entire game most worth spending budget on, since it plays at every one of the seven progression beats.

### 3.2 The Desync / Corruption Look
The rightmost figure in the reference sheet — softly faded, slightly mismatched proportions, a faint "glitch" sparkle rather than a clean glow — is canonized as CoCo's appearance during **any scene where her memory is actively being threatened or pulled at**: proximity to Static creatures, the Confluence and Loop boss fights if she's present, and critically, **during the Archivist's Phase Three choice in the finale**, where this desync look should intensify the longer the player hesitates, giving the final decision real, visible stakes on her body rather than only in dialogue.

### 3.3 The Fully-Rendered "Ideal" Model
The center, fully saturated, signature-tagged figure in the reference sheet is the cleanest single-image representation of "peak CoCo" and should be treated as the base master file all four other stages' proportions, face shape, and construction lines are built from — even though her actual in-game outfit at that exact saturation only fully appears at stage 8. Keep this file as the permanent proportion/construction reference for every artist who works on her going forward.

## 4. THE BOY PRESENTATION — PENDING ART (Text Spec for the Artist)

**Status: no concept art exists yet for this presentation.** Everything below is a written brief to hand the artist, not a description of finished work — treat it as the equivalent of the two reference sheets that anchored §1–§3, written in words instead of paint so nothing is lost between now and when art time is available.

**Design-language decision (assumption flagged):** whether the boy presentation should mirror the girl's silhouettes closely or use a distinct-but-matched design language has not yet been confirmed. In the absence of a confirmed answer, this spec defaults to **distinct but thematically matched** — same palette laws, same 8-stage growth logic, same prop-and-silhouette *language*, but its own construction rather than a simple recolor or gender-swap of the existing model. This keeps both presentations feeling like deliberate designs rather than making one read as the "real" version and the other as a reskin. **Flag to confirm before production art begins.**

**True name:** Cosimo (paired with Cosima per the main EDAS Game Design Bible, §4.2 and §5.0).

### 4.1 Shared Rules (apply to both presentations)
- Same 8-stage names and story beats: Novice → Explorer → Seeker → Apprentice → Scholar → Sage → Archivist → Keeper of Memories.
- Same core palette law: amethyst purple + cream/white + gold, gold coverage growing stage over stage.
- Same silhouette rule: legible in pure black silhouette at a glance.
- Same ability set per stage, same unlocks, same emotional beats per §2.1–2.8 above — none of that is gendered, all of it carries over directly.
- Same Transition Flicker (§3.1) and Desync/Corruption look (§3.2) VFX treatment.
- Same 7-expression sheet and its staging rules (no tears before Sage-equivalent, unguarded smile reserved for the final stage only).

### 4.2 Where This Presentation Diverges — Direction, Not Final Art
- **Hair:** same golden-blonde hue-lock as the girl presentation (this should never change between presentations — gold hair is core identity, not a gendered trait). Shorter and less voluminous at Novice, gaining length/complexity at the same *rate* the girl presentation gains dress complexity — by Keeper of Memories, hair should be dramatically longer/more elaborate than at Novice, mirroring the growth curve without mirroring the exact shape.
- **Silhouette base:** structured jacket-and-trouser or jacket-and-tunic base rather than a dress base, so the two presentations read as distinctly different silhouettes even at early stages, while using the *same* gold-trim-growth and cape-growth logic from Seeker onward.
- **Props:** identical prop *progression* (satchel → lantern → orbiting pages → held book → orb → free hands → staff), since props are tied to abilities, not gender — no divergence needed here at all.
- **Final stage:** same halo/aura treatment, same crown-motif payoff held back until stage 8, same full-radiance gold treatment — the ending's visual payoff must land with equal weight regardless of which presentation the player chose, since the True Ending doesn't change based on this choice.

### 4.3 Production Note
Because the girl presentation's model sheet is already approved, the fastest, most consistent path is to build the boy presentation's 8-stage turnaround directly off the same construction/proportion base described in §3.3, changing only silhouette, hairstyle shape, and garment construction — not eyes, not face shape, not palette law. This keeps both presentations recognizably part of the same character design family, which matters since players may see both in menus or marketing before choosing.

## 5. EXPRESSION SHEET (Universal, All Stages)

Seven expressions, reused across all 8 stages with only proportional/costume changes, to keep animation cost sane: **Neutral, Happy, Surprised, Thinking, Sad, Determined, Excited.**

**Canon rules for consistency:**
- Eyebrows do the majority of emotional work; mouth shapes stay relatively simple and reusable across expressions.
- **Sad** should never include visible tears before stage 6 (Sage) — earlier sadness reads as quiet stillness and a slight downward eye-angle, reserving actual tears as a late-game escalation with more weight.
- **Excited** and **Happy** should be visually distinct: Happy is closed/soft (small smile, relaxed eyes), Excited is open (visible teeth, wider eyes, small motion lines) — these get confused often in production and should be checked explicitly in every art pass.
- **Determined** is the one expression that should visibly change the most across stages — from a slightly-too-serious child's frown at Novice to a calm, settled resolve by Keeper of Memories — since it's the expression most tied to her growing self-trust.
- Reserve the **unguarded full-eyes-closed smile** (§2.8) exclusively for Keeper of Memories; using it earlier anywhere weakens its final payoff.

## 6. PRODUCTION NOTES FOR "FOREVER" USE

- **Treat this document as the single source of truth.** If a future concept pass wants to change a color, prop, or silhouette beat, that change gets written back into this document immediately — never left to live only in a Slack thread or a single artist's memory.
- **Master construction file:** build a proper turnaround/model sheet for the §3.3 "Fully-Rendered Ideal Model" first, then derive all 8 stages' costume changes from that single proportion base, rather than redrawing proportions at every stage.
- **Palette lock:** maintain one shared swatch file with the exact hex values used across all 8 stages (base purple, deep purple, trim gold, warm lantern-gold, cream, blush pink) so no stage ever drifts off-palette during production.
- **Naming consistency:** always use the exact stage names in this document (Novice, Explorer, Seeker, Apprentice, Scholar, Sage, Archivist, Keeper of Memories) in code, UI, and asset filenames — do not let "Luminary Path" or any earlier draft naming re-enter the pipeline; this document supersedes that naming.
- **Presentation & true-name consistency:** the character's in-play nickname is player-chosen at first meeting (default: CoCo) and never affects the fixed true-name reveal — **Cosima** for the girl presentation, **Cosimo** for the boy presentation. Keep these two facts — nickname vs. true name — separate in every asset filename and script variable, so localization and voiceover pipelines never collapse them into one field.
